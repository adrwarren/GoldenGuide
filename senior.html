YASSSSS KIDDOS... IT'S SENIOR SEASON!!!

You did it! But the ride doesn't end here...

Senior year is the moment you've been waiting for since your first day of high school. It's the end of an era, and the prelude to the rest of your life. It is definetly a year that you want to make count socially AND academically. But, before Senioritis attacks, let's get down to business and talk about the MUST KNOWS of senior year. It may seem a little scary, but don't worry: We got this!

The truth of the matter is...

This is really going to be your first big decision. And without help, you might not know all the facts to make an informed decision. So here's some basic tips to start the process.

  1. ALREADY HAVE YOUR ESSAY DONE. The earlier you have your essay done, the easier it is to apply. Write something sincere that really captivates your personality. College isn't just about the grades you get but the person you are.
  2. KNOW YOURSELF. Where you go to college should reflect how you want to live. Big city or small town? Big sports following? Local job opportunities? Ask yourself WHERE you want to be when you grow up so you know what to look for.
  3. IT'S OKAY NOT TO KNOW A CAREER. Don't be afraid to use college to explore your interests. You are going to be exposed to a lot more people and professions that you might find you like. Definitely don't get caught up in reputations. It doesn't matter where you go to college- everyone's path is there own. Ivy or trade doesn't matter unless you find yourself while there.
  4. START EARLY. END EARLY. College is important. But it shouldn't overshadow your senior year. When you finish everything early, you stress the little stuff less. AND, everyone looks at you in awe when you say you're done by October!
  5. HAVE FUN! Don't forget! It's your senior year. Take this time to spend time with your friends and enjoy your last year in high school. College definitely isn't the same as high school so enjoy the moments as they come.

    Tips for Finding Scholarships

  • Don't be afraid to ask. Start with your church, school,or local stores. You can even ask you siblings, friends, or counselors to discuss where students have gotten scholarships in the past.
  • KEEP APPLYING Apply to as many scholarships as possible. DON'T GIVE UP!!! You can send the same essays to multiple scholarships to save time.
  • Organize yourself. Make an Excel or Google Sheet and organize what colleges you've applied to, what colleges you're going to apply to, and what college application deadlines have passed. That way you don't miss opportunities and you keep track of your progress.

    Considering the Campus:

  • Take a tour of the campus. Whether it be virtual or in-person, take the time to figure out whether it feels right to you.
  • Go to webinars and panels. This can be a great way to learn about programs and opportunities that will be presented to you while on campus. It can also be a time to learn from current students and alumni what they liked and didn't like about their education.
  • Reach out to the admissions team and future professors. In the end, this is all about you. Emailing or scheduling a meeting with campus faculty can help you understand how classes will be taught and clear up any questions you may have. Don't be afraid! This is a big decision.
